Intel's Sustainability Journey

Building a more sustainable future through innovation.

1985

Early Adoption of Recyclable Materials

Began using recyclable materials in its packaging to minimize waste.

1995

Reducing Emissions with Innovative Cooling

Introduced new cooling technologies in facilities to cut greenhouse gas emissions significantly.

2008

Launching the First Green Building

Intel built its first LEED-certified green building, setting a benchmark for future facilities.

2012

Investing in Water Restoration Projects

Intel started funding community-based water restoration projects in regions experiencing scarcity.

2015

Joining the Climate Leadership Program

Intel joined the EPA's Climate Leadership Program, committing to ambitious goals for reducing greenhouse gas emissions.

2017

Advancing Energy-Efficient Processors

Intel released a new generation of processors designed to reduce energy consumption in data centers by 40%.

2019

Zero Waste Landfill Initiative

Intel implemented processes to ensure that 90% of waste generated in manufacturing facilities was recycled or reused.

2021

Achieving 100% Renewable Energy in U.S. Operations

Intel transitioned all its U.S. facilities to run entirely on renewable energy sources, reducing its carbon footprint significantly.

2023

Innovation in Water Conservation

Intel reduced water usage by 30% across global facilities.
